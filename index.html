About the Project

The Problem We're Solving

The Challenge

Brain tumors affect over 300,000 people globally each year. Manual diagnosis from MRI scans is time-consuming, error-prone, and requires highly skilled neuroradiologists — a scarce resource in developing nations.

  • Subjective interpretation leads to diagnostic variability
  • Manual segmentation takes 30–90 minutes per scan
  • Early-stage tumors are frequently missed
  • Limited access to expert radiologists in rural areas

Healthcare Importance

Early, accurate tumor detection directly impacts patient survival rates. A 5-year survival rate of 36% for glioblastoma underscores the urgency of early diagnosis and precise treatment planning.

  • Early detection improves survival by up to 40%
  • Precise segmentation enables targeted radiotherapy
  • AI-assisted diagnosis reduces clinician burnout
  • Standardizes diagnostic output across facilities

Project Objective

To develop a PBEDS (Patch-Based Ensemble Deep Segmentation) framework powered by CNN-based deep learning that can:

  • Automatically classify MRI scans as normal or tumor-positive
  • Identify tumor type: Glioma, Meningioma, Pituitary
  • Precisely segment tumor boundaries for treatment mapping
  • Deliver results in under 3 seconds with >98% accuracy
Methodology

PBEDS Pipeline

A structured, 6-stage deep learning workflow from raw MRI data to clinical-ready segmentation output.

01

Data Collection

Kaggle Brain MRI Tumor Dataset — 3,064 T1-weighted MRI images across 4 classes (Glioma, Meningioma, Pituitary, Normal). DICOM & JPEG formats.

02

Preprocessing

Skull stripping, image normalization (0–255 → 0–1), resizing to 224×224, data augmentation (rotation, flip, zoom), class balancing via SMOTE.

03

Patch Extraction (PBEDS)

Input MRI images are divided into overlapping 64×64 patches. Each patch is independently analyzed, enabling fine-grained spatial feature extraction.

04

Model Training (CNN Ensemble)

Ensemble of VGG-16, ResNet-50, and custom U-Net. Transfer learning with ImageNet weights. Adam optimizer, binary cross-entropy + dice loss. 80/20 train-test split.

05

Tumor Detection

Ensemble voting across patch predictions generates a heatmap. Bounding box localization identifies tumor region. Classification head outputs tumor type with confidence score.

06

Segmentation & Output

U-Net decoder reconstructs pixel-wise tumor mask. Post-processing with CRF refinement. Output includes segmentation overlay, metrics, and clinical report PDF.

Road Ahead for PBEDS

Hospital EHR Integration

Direct integration with Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems via HL7 FHIR API. Enables one-click analysis from within PACS/DICOM workstations used by radiologists.

Target: 2026 Q1

Mobile App Support

React Native cross-platform mobile app for field clinics. Offline inference using TensorFlow Lite quantized model — enabling tumor screening without internet in remote areas.

Target: 2026 Q2

Larger Dataset Training

Collaboration with 5 regional hospitals for de-identified MRI data pooling. Target: 50,000+ scans with federated learning to protect patient privacy while improving accuracy to 99.5%.

3D MRI Volume Analysis

Extend 2D slice analysis to full 3D volumetric segmentation using 3D U-Net. Enables tumor volume calculation and longitudinal tracking across treatment sessions.

Target: 2027 Q1
